Pneumonia Treatment Guidelines

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus, causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing.

Description:
Pneumonia ranges in seriousness from mild to life-threatening. It is most serious for infants and young children, people older than age 65, and people with health problems or weakened immune systems.

Treatment:
- Antibiotics for bacterial pneumonia (Amoxicillin 500mg three times daily)
- Rest and fluids to prevent dehydration
- Over-the-counter medications (Acetaminophen/Ibuprofen) for fever and pain
- Hospitalization may be required for severe cases or those with low oxygen levels

Prevention:
- Vaccination (Pneumococcal vaccine)
- Good hygiene (frequent handwashing)
- Healthy lifestyle (quitting smoking)
